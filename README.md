# Philosophies

Curated reading lists for the primary texts of Eastern and Western philosophical traditions, with links to the best free online editions and recommendations for paid translations.

## Contents

### Reading Lists

- **[Western Philosophy & Political Theory Canon](western_philosophy_canon.md)** — From the pre-Socratics through 20th-century analytic and continental philosophy. Covers Ancient Greek, Medieval (Christian, Islamic, Jewish), Early Modern, 19th-century, and 20th-century thinkers with links to Perseus, Gutenberg, MIT Classics, and other free archives.

- **[East Asian Philosophy Canon](east_asian_philosophy_canon.md)** — Chinese and Japanese philosophical traditions. Covers Confucianism, Daoism, Legalism, Chan/Zen Buddhism, Neo-Confucianism, Kokugaku, and the Kyoto School, with links to ctext.org, BDK, terebess.hu, and Archive.org.

### Timelines

- **[Western Philosophy Timeline](western_philosophy_canon_timeline.html)** — Visual timeline from the pre-Socratics to Foucault, color-coded by tradition.

- **[East Asian Philosophy Timeline](chinese_japanese_philosophy_timeline.html)** — Visual timeline from Confucius to Nishida, color-coded by Chinese and Japanese canon.

## Structure

Each reading list includes:

- **Free online links** for every text where available
- **Best paid translation** recommendations with translator and publisher
- **Minimal reading paths** for focused study
- **Key platforms** for further exploration

## Scope

The Western canon covers philosophy and political theory proper. The East Asian canon covers the Chinese and Japanese traditions. See the notes in each file for what's deliberately excluded and why.
