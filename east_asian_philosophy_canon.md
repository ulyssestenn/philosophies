## CHINESE CANON

### Essential Core (The Foundational Age)

**Confucius** — *Analects* (Lunyu)
- [ctext.org/analects](https://ctext.org/analects) — parallel Chinese/English, scholarly (best free option)
- [classics.mit.edu](http://classics.mit.edu/Confucius/analects.html) — clean English, Legge translation
- **Best paid translation:** D.C. Lau (Penguin Classics)

**Mencius** — *Mencius* (Mengzi)
- [ctext.org/mengzi](https://ctext.org/mengzi)
- **Best paid translation:** D.C. Lau (Penguin Classics)

**Xunzi** — *Xunzi*
- [ctext.org/xunzi](https://ctext.org/xunzi)
- **Best paid translation:** Burton Watson, *Xunzi: Basic Writings* (Columbia)

**Laozi** — *Tao Te Ching* (Daodejing)
- [ctext.org/dao-de-jing](https://ctext.org/dao-de-jing)
- [classics.mit.edu](http://classics.mit.edu/Lao/taote.html)
- **Best paid translations:** D.C. Lau (Penguin); Addiss & Lombardo (literary)

**Zhuangzi** — *Zhuangzi*
- [ctext.org/zhuangzi](https://ctext.org/zhuangzi)
- **Best paid translation:** Burton Watson, *The Complete Works of Zhuangzi* (Columbia) — widely considered the definitive English version

**Han Feizi** — *Han Feizi*
- [ctext.org/hanfeizi](https://ctext.org/hanfeizi)
- **Best paid translation:** Burton Watson, *Han Feizi: Basic Writings* (Columbia)

**Mozi** — *Mozi*
- [ctext.org/mozi](https://ctext.org/mozi)
- **Best paid translation:** Burton Watson, *Mo Tzu: Basic Writings* (Columbia)

### Second Tier (Buddhist & Neo-Confucian)

**Huineng** — *Platform Sutra*
- [BDK/McRae translation (free PDF)](https://www.bdk.or.jp/document/dgtl-dl/dBET_T2008_PlatformSutra_2000.pdf) — scholarly, widely respected
- [Red Pine translation on terebess.hu (free PDF)](https://terebess.hu/zen/platform-sutra.pdf)
- **Best paid translation:** Philip Yampolsky (Columbia) — definitive scholarly edition; Red Pine (Counterpoint) — most readable

**Fazang** — *Treatise on the Golden Lion*; *Treatise on the Five Teachings*
- [Fazang "Golden Lion" translation (PhilArchive)](https://philarchive.org/archive/WONTFO) — annotated English translation
- [Fazang writings overview & links (academic site)](https://sites.google.com/uah.edu/fazang/home/fazangs-writings)
- [Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy — Huayan Buddhism](https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/buddhism-huayan/) — excellent philosophical overview
- **Best paid:** Garma C.C. Chang, *The Buddhist Teaching of Totality* (includes Golden Lion translation)

**Zhu Xi** — *Commentaries on the Four Books*
- [Great Learning (ctext.org)](https://ctext.org/liji/da-xue)
- [Doctrine of the Mean (ctext.org)](https://ctext.org/liji/zhong-yong)
- **Best paid:** Daniel K. Gardner, *Learning to Be a Sage* (selections from Zhu Xi); Wing-tsit Chan, *Reflections on Things at Hand*

**Wang Yangming** — *Instructions for Practical Living* (Chuanxi Lu)
- [ctext.org/wang-yangming](https://ctext.org/wang-yangming)
- **Best paid translation:** Wing-tsit Chan (Columbia) — the standard English edition

**Dong Zhongshu** — *Luxuriant Dew of the Spring and Autumn Annals*
- [ctext.org/chun-qiu-fan-lu](https://ctext.org/chun-qiu-fan-lu)

### Specialized / Additional

**Sima Qian** — *Records of the Grand Historian* (Shiji)
- [Archive.org — Qin Dynasty volume (Watson trans.)](https://archive.org/details/recordsofgrandhi0000sima_y7t5)
- [Archive.org — Han Dynasty volumes (Watson trans.)](https://archive.org/details/recordsofgrandhi0000sima)
- **Best paid translation:** Burton Watson, 3-volume set (Columbia University Press) — the standard

**Huang Zongxi** — *Waiting for the Dawn* (Mingyi Daifang Lu)
- [ctext.org (partial)](https://ctext.org/wiki.pl?if=en&res=857684)
- **Best paid:** Wm. Theodore de Bary, *Waiting for the Dawn* (Columbia)

**Later Mohists** — *Mohist Canons* (logic & epistemology)
- **Best (and essentially only) serious English treatment:** A.C. Graham, *Later Mohist Logic, Ethics and Science* — no free version, but uniquely valuable for anyone interested in formal logic in Chinese thought

## JAPANESE CANON

### Essential Core

**Dōgen** — *Shōbōgenzō* (Treasury of the True Dharma Eye)
- [Nishijima/Cross translation Vol. 1 (free PDF, BDK)](https://www.bdk.or.jp/document/dgtl-dl/dBET_T2582_Shobogenzo1_2009.pdf)
- [Vol. 2](https://www.bdk.or.jp/document/dgtl-dl/dBET_T2582_Shobogenzo2_2008.pdf)
- [Vol. 3](https://www.bdk.or.jp/document/dgtl-dl/dBET_T2582_Shobogenzo3_2008.pdf)
- [Vol. 4](https://www.bdk.or.jp/document/dgtl-dl/dBET_T2582_Shobogenzo4_2008.pdf)
- [Authorised Windbell edition (free ebooks)](https://www.shobogenzo.net/index.php/text-1/authorised-version/)
- **Best paid translation:** Kazuaki Tanahashi, *Treasury of the True Dharma Eye* (Shambhala) — most readable and literary

**Kūkai** — *Major Works* (including *Attaining Enlightenment in This Very Existence*, *The Precious Key to the Secret Treasury*, *The Meanings of Sound, Word, and Reality*)
- No complete free translation available online
- **Best (and essential) paid translation:** Yoshito S. Hakeda, *Kūkai: Major Works* (Columbia University Press) — the definitive English collection

**Shinran** — *Tannishō* (Lamenting the Deviations); *Kyōgyōshinshō*
- [Tannishō (free, multiple translations on terebess.hu)](https://terebess.hu/zen/mesterek/Tannisho.pdf)
- **Best paid:** Dennis Hirota et al., *The Collected Works of Shinran* (Jodo Shinshu Hongwanji-ha)

**Motoori Norinaga** — *Commentaries on the Kojiki*
- The essential background text: [Kojiki, Chamberlain translation (Archive.org)](https://archive.org/details/kojikioraccounto00cham)
- **Best paid Kojiki:** Gustav Heldt (Columbia)
- Norinaga's own commentaries remain only partially translated into English

**Nishida Kitarō** — *An Inquiry into the Good* (Zen no Kenkyū)
- [Archive.org listing (borrowable)](https://archive.org/details/inquiryintogood0000nish)
- **Best paid translation:** Masao Abe & Christopher Ives (Yale University Press, 1990) — the standard

## KEY PLATFORMS

- **[ctext.org](https://ctext.org)** — Chinese Text Project. The essential platform for Chinese philosophy. Parallel Chinese/English, fully searchable. The Perseus equivalent for Chinese texts.
- **[terebess.hu](https://terebess.hu/zen/zen.html)** — Best consolidated free resource for Zen/Chan Buddhist texts in English.
- **[BDK Digital Library](https://www.bdk.or.jp/en/english-tripitaka/digital-downloads.html)** — Free scholarly Buddhist text translations (PDF).
- **[Archive.org](https://archive.org)** — Public domain and borrowable translations of many texts listed above.

## MINIMAL READING PATHS

**Chinese philosophy in 6 texts (the battlefield):**
1. Analects (Confucius)
2. Mencius
3. Xunzi
4. Tao Te Ching (Laozi)
5. Zhuangzi
6. Han Feizi

**Japanese philosophy in 5 texts (the divergence from China):**
1. Shōbōgenzō, selected chapters (Dōgen)
2. Kūkai: Major Works (Hakeda translation)
3. Tannishō (Shinran)
4. Kojiki (with Norinaga context)
5. An Inquiry into the Good (Nishida)
