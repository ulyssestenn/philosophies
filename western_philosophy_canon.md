## SCOPE & STRUCTURE

This covers philosophy and political theory — the thinkers whose ideas shaped the conceptual architecture of Western civilization. I've organized by period and internal logic rather than by arbitrary "rank." Literary figures (Homer, Dante, Shakespeare) and pure scientists (Newton, Darwin) appear only where their work is directly philosophical.

## I. ANCIENT GREEK PHILOSOPHY

### The Pre-Socratics (Origins of Systematic Thought)

The pre-Socratics survive only in fragments. No single free-standing text exists for most of them. The essential modern collection is:

- **Best paid:** G.S. Kirk, J.E. Raven & M. Schofield, *The Presocratic Philosophers* (Cambridge) — the standard scholarly edition with fragments and commentary
- **Alternative:** Daniel W. Graham, *The Texts of Early Greek Philosophy* (Cambridge) — more complete, more recent

Key figures: **Heraclitus**, **Parmenides**, **Democritus**, **Anaximander**, **Empedocles**

## VI. KEY PLATFORMS

- **[Perseus Digital Library](https://www.perseus.tufts.edu/hopper/)** — The essential platform for Greek and Roman texts. Parallel Greek/English, fully searchable. The ctext.org equivalent for Western antiquity.
- **[MIT Internet Classics Archive](http://classics.mit.edu/)** — Cleaner reading interface for Greek/Roman texts, good translations.
- **[Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/)** — Massive public-domain library. Most Enlightenment and 19th-century philosophy is here.
- **[Marxists.org](https://www.marxists.org/archive/)** — Best free archive for Marx, Engels, and related thinkers (also hosts Hegel, Sartre, and others).
- **[New Advent](https://www.newadvent.org/)** — Complete Summa Theologica and Church Fathers.
- **[Early Modern Texts](https://www.earlymoderntexts.com/)** — Jonathan Bennett's modernized-language versions of Descartes, Hume, Kant, Leibniz, Locke, etc. Excellent for first readings.
- **[Archive.org](https://archive.org/)** — Public domain and borrowable editions of nearly everything.

## VII. MINIMAL READING PATHS

**Ancient philosophy in 5 texts:**
1. Plato, *Republic*
2. Plato, *Apology* + *Phaedo* (read together)
3. Aristotle, *Nicomachean Ethics*
4. Aristotle, *Politics* (selections)
5. Marcus Aurelius, *Meditations*

**The political theory canon in 7 texts:**
1. Plato, *Republic*
2. Aristotle, *Politics*
3. Machiavelli, *The Prince*
4. Hobbes, *Leviathan*
5. Locke, *Second Treatise*
6. Rousseau, *The Social Contract*
7. Mill, *On Liberty*

**The epistemology/metaphysics arc in 6 texts:**
1. Plato, *Theaetetus* or *Meno*
2. Descartes, *Meditations*
3. Hume, *Enquiry Concerning Human Understanding*
4. Kant, *Critique of Pure Reason* (or the *Prolegomena* as entry point)
5. Wittgenstein, *Tractatus* then *Philosophical Investigations*
6. Quine, "Two Dogmas of Empiricism"

**The existentialist arc in 4 texts:**
1. Kierkegaard, *Fear and Trembling*
2. Nietzsche, *On the Genealogy of Morality*
3. Heidegger, *Being and Time* (Division I)
4. Sartre, *Existentialism Is a Humanism* (then *Being and Nothingness* if committed)

## VIII. A NOTE ON WHAT'S NOT HERE

This list deliberately excludes several important categories that border philosophy but have their own canons:

**Theology proper:** The Church Fathers (Origen, Gregory of Nyssa, John Chrysostom), Reformation thinkers (Luther, Calvin), and modern theology (Barth, Tillich, Bonhoeffer) — these matter enormously but constitute a separate tradition.

**Philosophy of science beyond Kuhn:** Popper, Lakatos, Feyerabend, van Fraassen — important but specialized.

**Feminist philosophy:** Wollstonecraft's *Vindication of the Rights of Woman* (1792, [Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/3420)), de Beauvoir's *The Second Sex*, and Butler's *Gender Trouble* all belong on an expanded list. Wollstonecraft especially deserves to be read alongside her contemporaries.

**African-American philosophy:** Frederick Douglass, W.E.B. Du Bois (*The Souls of Black Folk*, [Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/408)), James Baldwin — each produced work that functions as philosophy even when not labeled as such.

**Pragmatism:** William James, John Dewey, C.S. Peirce — the most distinctly American philosophical contribution, underrepresented in most "Western canon" lists.

These omissions are about scope, not judgment. Any of these could anchor a canon of its own.
